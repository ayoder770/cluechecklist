Who is Team Gas Aware?

Team Gas Aware is a Georgia Tech ECE Senior Design Group working with the ATSDR(Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry) and the CDC (Center for Disease Control) to create a more efficient and safer system to detect toxic levels of hydrogen sulfide gas (H2S) in the atmosphere. Currently, human lab technicians are deployed to the field and use their sense of smell to detect toxic gas levels. This method needs to be replaced for several reasons:

  • This method is expensive as lab techs are paid to out to the field, as well as test gas samples that may not produce any valuable results
  • Time is wasted going to test areas that may or may not contain dangerous levels of H2S
  • Safety of the lab technicians is a concern, as well as the inhabitants of the area who may not even be aware there is a toxic level of H2S gas around them.

What Is the Project?

Team GasAware will be building three sensing units using Arduinos. Each unit will include an H2S sensor, a wifi chip, and an LCD screen. The sensor will take readings for H2S levels in the atmosphere and will transmit the readings to a server via wifi using the wifi chip. The screen on each unit will display the current reading of H2S. One of the three units will contain a gas canister that will take a phsical gas sample when a toxic level of H2S is detected by the server. The server will be responsible for alerting a lab tech via text message to go collect the sample and take it to the lab for analysis.

The Advisors

Georgia Tech Faculty Advisor: Dr. William Hunt

Team GasAware is being advised by Georgia Tech Faculty member Dr. William Hunt. Dr. Hunt received his B.S.E.E. from the University of Alabama in 1976, his S.M.E.E. in 1980 from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and his Ph.D. in electrical engineering from University of Illinois, Champaign-Urbana in 1987. During his career in industry, Dr. Hunt worked at Harris Corporation, Bolt, and Beranek and Newman, Inc. In 1987, Dr. Hunt joined the Georgia Tech faculty as one of the original members of the Pettit Microsystems Research Center where he founded the Microelectronic Acoustics Group. Dr. Hunt's primary areas of interest are thin film piezoelectric materials, SAW and BAW devices for wireless applications, Application of microelectronic acoustics to chemical sensing and biological research, and Device physics and fabrication of microelectronic acoustic devices.
